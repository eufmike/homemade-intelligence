## Vision

Build a **personal, unbiased intelligence platform** that synthesizes geopolitical and financial information from diverse global sources — producing actionable analysis reports tailored to multiple audiences, with a particular emphasis on **US-interested topics** and **East Asian dynamics including Taiwan strait safety**.

This is not a news aggregator. It is a structured analytical system designed to:

- **Triangulate** across ideologically and geographically distinct sources
- **Detect bias** rather than pretend it doesn't exist
- **Generate reports** in formats appropriate for different audiences and literacy levels
- **Bridge the information gap** between English-language and Mandarin-language or any other language geopolitical discourse

---

## Problem Statement

1. **Mainstream media consolidation** creates blind spots — no single outlet covers the full picture of US politics, East Asian security, and global financial markets without editorial bias.
2. **Language barriers** prevent Mandarin-speaking audiences (especially in Taiwan) from accessing high-quality, triangulated geopolitical analysis typically available only in English.
3. **Generational accessibility gaps** mean that critical information about Taiwan strait safety, cross-strait relations, and regional security never reaches the people most affected — including Taiwanese elders (65+) who rely on traditional media ecosystems vulnerable to information manipulation.
4. **Investment decisions** are routinely made without adequate consideration of geopolitical risk — particularly energy chokepoint exposure, compound vulnerability windows, and non-Western perspectives on market-moving events.

## Goals

### Goal 1 — Unbiased Personal Intelligence Platform

Build a structured analytical system for **political analysis and investment analysis** that:

- Aggregates information from **ideologically diverse sources** (left, right, center, non-Western, specialist)
- Applies **triangulation methodology** — no single source treated as authoritative
- Tracks **narrative manipulation** and coordinated inauthentic behavior (CIB)
- Provides **financial market early warning signals** alongside geopolitical analysis
- Monitors **energy markets, commodity flows, and chokepoint intelligence** as primary geopolitical-economic transmission mechanisms
- Implements the **Compound Vulnerability Framework** for anticipatory analysis
- Builds **politician profiling** — systematic behavioral analysis of key political figures (decision patterns, grievance cycles, donor/ally relationships, stated vs. revealed preferences) to enable predictive modeling of policy actions and leadership decisions
- **Captures rare events** (black swans, geopolitical shocks, market dislocations) and provides structured **political and economic explanations** — including root-cause analysis, compound trigger identification, precedent comparison, and cascading-impact assessment across affected domains

### Goal 2 — Broadened Geographic Scope: US + East Asia

Cover both **US-interested topics** and **East Asian dynamics**, specifically:

| Domain | Coverage Areas |
|--------|---------------|
| **US Politics** | Policy analysis, Trump behavior prediction, legislative tracking, institutional dynamics |
| **US-China Relations** | Trade policy, technology competition, diplomatic signaling, military posture |
| **Taiwan Strait Safety** | Cross-strait military activity, PLA exercises, US arms sales, diplomatic status, economic coercion signals |
| **East Asian Security** | Japan-Korea dynamics, ASEAN positioning, South China Sea, North Korea, semiconductor supply chain security |
| **Regional Economics** | TSMC and semiconductor geopolitics, Asia-Pacific trade flows, Strait of Malacca and regional chokepoint monitoring |
| **Information Warfare** | Chinese state media narrative tracking (Xinhua, Global Times), CIB campaigns targeting Taiwan, cognitive warfare assessment |

#### Taiwan Strait Safety — Special Focus

Taiwan strait safety is treated as a **first-class analytical domain**, not a subtopic of US-China relations. This includes:

- **Military dimension**: PLA naval and air activity, median line crossings, live-fire exercises, amphibious capability assessments
- **Economic dimension**: Trade dependency risks, semiconductor leverage, ECFA (Economic Cooperation Framework Agreement) status, tourism and people-to-people flows
- **Diplomatic dimension**: International space contraction/expansion, ally support signals, UN-adjacent participation
- **Information warfare dimension**: Disinformation campaigns targeting Taiwanese public opinion, deepfake and synthetic media operations, election interference indicators
- **Civilian preparedness dimension**: Civil defense readiness, critical infrastructure resilience, energy import vulnerability

### Goal 3 — Multi-Audience Report Generation

Generate intelligence reports tailored to **three distinct audience profiles**:

#### Audience 1: English Speakers 🇺🇸🌐

- **Language**: English
- **Format**: Full analytical reports with source citations, confidence levels, and methodology notes
- **Tone**: Professional, analytical — comparable to think tank publications
- **Depth**: Complete triangulation evidence, alternative interpretations, and dissenting-view sections
- **Use case**: Personal decision-making, investment analysis, informed citizenship

#### Audience 2: Mandarin Speakers in Taiwan 🇹🇼

- **Language**: Traditional Chinese (繁體中文) — Taiwan standard
- **Format**: Structured briefings with clear section headers, source attribution translated or annotated
- **Tone**: Informative and balanced — explicitly noting where Western and local Taiwanese media narratives diverge
- **Depth**: Full analytical depth with Taiwan-specific context added (e.g., local political party positions, historical precedent from prior cross-strait crises)
- **Cultural notes**: Avoid PRC-standard simplified Chinese; use Taiwan-standard terminology (e.g., 總統 not 領導人 for Taiwan's president); respect local political sensitivities without taking sides
- **Use case**: Informed Taiwanese citizens seeking international-grade analysis in their native language

#### Audience 3: Taiwanese Elders (65+) 👴👵

- **Language**: Traditional Chinese (繁體中文) — simplified vocabulary, shorter sentences
- **Format**: Digest-style summaries — bullet points, key takeaways, clear "what this means for you" sections
- **Tone**: Warm, respectful, reassuring where factually appropriate — never condescending
- **Accessibility features**:
  - Larger text formatting recommendations
  - Avoidance of jargon — all technical terms explained in plain language on first use
  - "Traffic light" risk indicators (🟢 安全 / 🟡 注意 / 🔴 警戒) for intuitive comprehension
  - Audio-friendly structure (designed to be read aloud by family members or text-to-speech)
  - Focus on **"so what" implications** — how does this event affect daily life, savings, family safety?
- **Special considerations**:
  - This audience is disproportionately targeted by LINE group misinformation and CIB campaigns
  - Reports should include a "misinformation alert" section (謠言警示) addressing common false claims circulating on LINE, Facebook, and Taiwanese TV talk shows
  - Information about Taiwan strait safety should be presented with historical context to avoid unnecessary alarm
- **Use case**: Helping elderly family members access trustworthy, digestible information about the issues that affect them most

## Core Principles

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Triangulation over trust** | No single source is authoritative. Cross-reference across ideologically and geographically distinct outlets. |
| **Bias as a variable** | Every source has a perspective. The platform tracks and labels bias — it doesn't pretend objectivity exists. |
| **Leading over lagging** | Prioritize leading indicators (VIX, CDS, PMI, tanker flows) over lagging confirmations (GDP, official statements). |
| **Non-Western perspectives required** | Every analysis must include at least one non-Anglophone source to avoid epistemic capture. |
| **Audience-first design** | Reports are shaped by who reads them, not by what's easiest to produce. An elder in Taipei and an analyst in DC need different outputs from the same intelligence. |
| **Manipulation awareness** | Every report considers whether the underlying information may be subject to coordinated manipulation. |
| **Transparency of method** | Reports include confidence levels, source lists, and methodology notes — readers can evaluate the analysis, not just consume it. |

The project maintains a comprehensive **International Situation Intelligence Stack** (v2.0, March 2026) — a professionally structured source architecture covering 10 analytical layers. Version 2.0 was updated following the analytical stress-test performed during the February 28, 2026 US-Israel joint strike on Iran (Operation Epic Fury), which exposed critical gaps in energy intelligence, Persian-language sources, and compound vulnerability analysis.

#### Key Analytical Frameworks (v2.0)

- **Compound Vulnerability Framework** — Scores target states across military, economic, and political dimensions to identify windows of maximum exposure (validated by Iran strike timing analysis)
- **Behavioral Heuristics for Trump Decision Prediction** — 7 empirically observed patterns including *market crash → policy reversal* (primary constraint) and *negotiation as cover for military planning* (new in v2.0)
- **Real-Time Manipulation Detection Framework** — 9-signal checklist for identifying coordinated or AI-generated manipulation before acting on information
- **Chokepoint Monitoring Quick-Reference** — 5 critical maritime chokepoints with commodity flows, risk actors, and real-time monitoring sources
- **Analytical Failure Mode Reference** — 7 documented failure modes (Anglophone epistemic capture, negotiation optimism bias, nuclear pretext conflation, monocausal attribution, recency bias, confirmation bias, energy market blindspot)
